## How I Approach Testing? 
If you asked someone who has worked with me, they would probably say that I'm more interested in understanding why something happens than simply proving that it works.

I tend to focus on behavior rather than syntax, investigate unexpected results, and look closely at the assumptions hidden behind a test. I'm particularly interested in user experience, accessibility, runtime behavior, and the kinds of edge cases that can easily be overlooked.

One idea has become central to how I think about testing: Passing tests are useful. Understanding what they actually prove is even more important.

I rarely stop at: "The test passed." Instead, I find myself asking:
- What is this really validating?
- Could this pass for the wrong reason?
- Is this actually meaningful?
- What perspective is this using?
- What hidden assumption exists?
  
Those questions influence the way I approach both manual and automated testing. They help me design tests that are not only functional, but also intentional, maintainable, and trustworthy.
